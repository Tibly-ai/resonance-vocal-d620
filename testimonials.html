From First Lesson to First Solo · 14 Months
Foundations of Voice

Danielle, 34 — the singer who only sang in her car

When Danielle booked her trial lesson, she asked if she could face away from the piano "so nobody has to look at me while I make noise." Fourteen months later, she sang "The Prayer" as the solo at her sister's wedding — off-book, mic in hand, in front of 180 guests.

"Mara never once made me feel like a beginner was a bad thing to be. She made it feel like the most exciting thing to be."

Danielle's turning point came in month four, when breath-support work finally clicked and her wobble on sustained notes disappeared. She now sings with a community choir and is working on her mixed voice.

Danielle R. Foundations student, now in her third year

Vocal Recovery to Weekly Gigs · 9 Months
Vocal Health & Recovery → Contemporary & Pop

Marcus, 29 — rebuilding a belt that was falling apart

Marcus arrived on a referral from his ENT after two years of hoarseness, cancelled shows, and a scare with vocal fold swelling. His band was on hiatus and he was quietly convinced his singing days were over.

"I thought I was coming in to learn how to protect what was left. Instead I ended up with more range and more stamina than I had before the injury."

Working alongside his speech-language pathologist, we rebuilt his technique from gentle semi-occluded exercises up to a fully supported, sustainable belt. The Hollow Pines are back to three gigs a week — and Marcus warms up before every one.

Marcus T. Lead vocalist, The Hollow Pines

Two Acceptances, One Scholarship · 7 Months
Audition & Performance Prep

Priya, 18 — from callback nerves to a BFA scholarship

Priya had the voice; what she didn't have was a plan. Her audition book was three songs deep, her cuts ran long, and her nerves had derailed two callbacks the previous season. We spent seven months building a twelve-song book, drilling 16- and 32-bar cuts, and rehearsing the walk into the room as carefully as the songs themselves.

"By audition week, the room felt smaller than the studio. That's when I knew the prep had worked."

Priya was accepted to two of her three top-choice musical theatre programs, including a talent scholarship at the one she now attends.

Priya S. BFA Musical Theatre, Class of 2027
