About the Studio

The story behind Resonance

A performer's ear, a scientist's curiosity, and fifteen years of watching singers surprise themselves. Meet the coach and the philosophy at the heart of the studio.

♪ ♫
"I don't teach people to sing. I help them stop getting in the way of the voice they already have."
Meet Your Coach

Mara Ellison — soprano, pedagogue, professional voice geek

Mara grew up singing in church basements and community theatres in the Willamette Valley, then spent twelve years performing professionally — as a soprano soloist with regional opera companies, a session vocalist in Nashville, and a touring backing singer who learned firsthand what eight shows a week does to an unprepared voice.

That last lesson changed everything. After a bout of vocal fatigue nearly ended her career at 29, Mara rebuilt her own technique from the ground up under master teachers in New York and Chicago — and discovered she loved the rebuilding more than the performing. She earned her Master of Music in Vocal Pedagogy, opened Resonance Vocal Studio in 2010, and has been helping singers avoid her mistakes (and find their sound faster than she found hers) ever since.

Today she teaches a full studio of beginners, gigging professionals, theatre kids, choir directors, and retirees who always wanted to try. She still performs several recitals a year — because a teacher who stops singing stops understanding.

Training Philosophy

Good singing isn't a mystery — it's a skill

Everything we do in the studio rests on four convictions. If a lesson ever drifts from these, we've drifted from the point.

🔬

Science Over Superstition

Vocal folds don't respond to vague imagery alone. We pair time-tested bel canto tradition with modern voice science, so you understand what's happening in your body — and can reproduce it on demand, not just on lucky days.

🌿

Health Before Volume

A voice that hurts is a voice giving feedback. Every belt, every high note, every stylistic choice is built on sustainable coordination first. Your voice should feel better after singing, not worse.

🎨

Technique Serves Artistry

Scales and exercises are scaffolding, not the building. The goal is always the song — your interpretation, your emotion, your sound. We never sand off the qualities that make your voice recognizably yours.

🤝

Curiosity, Not Criticism

Fear tightens the throat faster than any bad habit. Lessons here are experiments, not exams — a place where a cracked note is data, not failure, and progress is measured against your own last month, nobody else's.
